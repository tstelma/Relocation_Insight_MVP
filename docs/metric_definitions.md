# Metric Definitions

- **inflation_pressure**: Annual inflation rate from Eurostat HICP. Lower is generally better because slower price growth reduces financial pressure.
- **housing_pressure**: Housing overburden rate from Eurostat SILC. Lower is generally better because fewer households spend more than 40% of income on housing.
- **poverty_pressure**: At-risk-of-poverty rate from Eurostat SILC. Lower is generally better because fewer people fall below 60% of median income.
- **income_capacity**: Median equivalised net income from Eurostat ilc_di03. Measured in Purchasing Power Standard (PPS). Higher is better because it indicates stronger local income capacity and purchasing power.

This MVP deliberately treats the first three metrics as pressure indicators and income capacity as a complementary, higher-is-better signal.
